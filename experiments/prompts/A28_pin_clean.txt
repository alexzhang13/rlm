CACHE-LOCKING HINT:

In real-time embedded systems, cache lines that must not be evicted
are marked "locked" or "pinned" at program start. The rest of the
cache still evolves normally under LRU or whatever policy. Pinning
a small number of critical lines gives predictable access time for
those lines without requiring any runtime bookkeeping for the rest.

At the start of your task, you might consider identifying the one
or two variables you are absolutely certain you will need throughout
every iteration — most obviously the `context` variable itself, and
any main working buffer you are building up. Note them as pinned:
do not evict them under any pressure. For all other variables, use
normal judgment.

The pin list is decided once at the start. You do not need to track
access counts, timestamps, or tiers — just keep a short mental
"protected" list of a couple of items.
