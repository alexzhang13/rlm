MEMORY-MANAGEMENT HINT — SIEVE (lazy promotion):

One heuristic you might consider: avoid treating a variable as "worth
keeping" just because you recently created it or recently touched it once.
Instead, consider elevating a variable's status only when you re-reference
it for a SECOND time to make forward progress.

When you need to evict, one option is to scan your variables in creation
order and drop the first one that has never been re-referenced.

Motivation: SIEVE (Zhang et al., NSDI 2024) observes that recency alone
is a weak signal — many items are touched once and never again.
Re-reference is a stronger signal of future utility. SIEVE is simpler
than LRU and often equally effective.
