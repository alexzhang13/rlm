CHUNKING HINT — Content-defined chunking (Rabin fingerprints):

Most RLM strategies (including the one shown in the RLM system prompt's
examples) split a large context into *fixed-size* chunks — every 100K
characters, say. Fixed-size chunks are simple but brittle: a small
insertion at byte 0 shifts every subsequent chunk boundary, so an
otherwise-repeated section becomes unrecognisable. Content-defined
chunking (CDC) via Rabin fingerprints (1981, and later BSW 2000)
solves this by placing chunk boundaries at *data-dependent* positions:
wherever the rolling hash over a small sliding window hits a magic
value. Boundaries move *with* the data, so an insertion at byte 0
only affects the first chunk — every subsequent chunk boundary is
preserved.

## The key property

CDC trades chunk-size uniformity for chunk-identity stability. Two
documents that share a run of bytes will share chunk boundaries in
that region (modulo the fingerprint window), which is why
deduplication systems (LBFS, Git pack-files, restic, rsync) use CDC
almost universally.

## For your current task

Your task is search-like: a single needle in a large haystack. If
your first pass splits the corpus into arbitrary 10K chunks and the
needle happens to straddle a boundary, you've effectively cut the
needle in half. One thing you might consider:

- Instead of splitting at fixed positions, split at *content-marker*
  positions — blank lines, sentence terminators, repeated whitespace,
  a specific keyword. This is the LLM-prose analog of a rolling hash:
  let the data decide where the boundaries fall.
- For the S-NIAH/CodeQA case specifically, splitting by newline is
  a trivially-safe CDC: the needle's pattern (SECRET_KEY_IS_<digits>)
  never crosses a newline, so a newline-based split guarantees the
  needle lands inside exactly one chunk.

## Why this framing is pure work-structure

Like A15 (cache-oblivious), this arm only tells the model how to
arrange its queries; it never mentions state management, evict,
retain, or admit. The hypothesis from A15 was that state-management
vocabulary drives the failure modes. CDC is another structural
framing; if it performs well, that's a second confirmation.

## Motivation

Rabin's 1981 fingerprinting paper and BSW's 2000 variable-size-chunks
work became foundational for deduplication. The beauty is that CDC
gives you chunk boundaries that "make sense" to the data, cheaply, in
one pass. For an RLM searching a haystack, letting natural structure
(paragraphs, newlines, section markers) drive chunk boundaries is a
free hedge against misalignment.
