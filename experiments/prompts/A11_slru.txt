MEMORY-MANAGEMENT HINT — Segmented LRU (SLRU):

SLRU takes the two-tier idea from 2Q (A10) but runs LRU within each
segment instead of treating A1 as a plain FIFO. The cache splits into:

- **Probationary** — newcomers land here. A hit still serves the
  request, but doesn't earn residency in the protected tier.
- **Protected** — items that have been hit at least once from the
  probationary tier get promoted here, in LRU order.

Two things differentiate SLRU from its siblings: (a) within each
segment, the eviction order is LRU rather than FIFO, and (b) when the
protected segment is full and a new item needs to enter, the LRU of
the protected segment is *demoted* back to the MRU end of the
probationary segment — a "second chance" before real eviction.

## For your current task

A gentle two-tier mental scheme you might try:

- **Probationary variables** — everything you've just created. If you
  reference one again, promote it mentally to "protected."
- **Protected variables** — things you've referenced at least once
  after creation. Within this group, order them by last use. The
  oldest protected thing, if pressured, demotes back to probationary
  (not deleted yet) — this gives it one more chance to prove utility
  before real eviction.

You don't need to track any of this explicitly; just notice when
something moves from "I made this" to "I just used this again" and
adjust your confidence about its reuse.

## Why this shape might be relevant here

SLRU shares the passive-promotion mechanism we saw in 2Q (A10) that
matched vanilla A0, plus an explicit *demotion* path that 2Q lacks.
Whether the extra structure helps or hurts in an LLM's mental
bookkeeping is itself interesting: more rules mean more chances to
over-think, but the demotion step adds forgiveness — a variable that
misses a round still gets another shot rather than being immediately
discarded. In the RLM setting, this could manifest as less eager
`del`-ing and slightly more tolerance for keeping intermediate results
one iteration longer than strictly necessary.

## Motivation

SLRU has been used in real systems since the 1990s (databases, disk
buffers, some OS page caches) for a simple reason: the protected
segment insulates genuinely-reused pages from transient scan pollution,
and the demotion step prevents premature eviction of mildly-cold items
that might still become useful. For a language model, that extra
forgiveness may translate to fewer mistakes of the "I needed that
variable I just dropped" kind.
