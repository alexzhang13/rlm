MEMORY-MANAGEMENT HINT — CLOCK-Pro (Jiang, Chen, Zhang 2005):

CLOCK-Pro (USENIX ATC 2005) is the LIRS-inspired upgrade of CLOCK:
same constant-time constant-space properties, but with a three-way
distinction between pages (HOT / COLD-resident / COLD-nonresident)
and two hands that sweep at different speeds. Like CLOCK (A9), it
avoids the ordered-list overhead of LRU; like LIRS (A21), it
separates frequently-hit items from transient scans. Unlike either,
it also remembers a small set of *non-resident* cold pages so it
can recognize a returning item and re-admit it quickly.

## The three-hand picture

- **Hand-hot** sweeps looking for hot pages whose reference bit is 0
  (they've gone cold) and demotes them to cold-resident.
- **Hand-cold** sweeps looking for a cold-resident page with ref=0
  to evict. If a cold-resident page has ref=1 (was accessed since
  last sweep) it gets promoted to hot instead.
- **Hand-test** runs ahead of hand-cold to purge stale entries in
  the non-resident list so it doesn't grow unbounded.

The paper shows this machinery produces LIRS-level hit rates with
CLOCK's implementation simplicity — a practical sweet spot.

## For your current task

The CLOCK-Pro mental model is three-state per variable:

- **hot** — actively in use, currently referenced.
- **cold-resident** — in your REPL but not recently touched.
- **cold-nonresident** — deleted but remembered by a short memo
  so that if you re-derive something similar, you can recognize
  it.

This arm deliberately layers three abstractions: the sweep metaphor
(from A9 CLOCK), the ghost-list idea (from A3 S3-FIFO), and the
binary hot/cold split (from A22). All three previously failed in
isolation. CLOCK-Pro combines them intelligently in production
systems but puts heavy cognitive load on an LLM trying to maintain
the mental bookkeeping.

## Prediction

Per the refined hypothesis (state-management fails, especially when
expensive), A23 should either FAIL (runs but wrong) or CRASH (times
out). It combines the failure modes of three predecessor arms:
over-management (A9), multi-tier (A11 SLRU), and binary categorization
(A22). If it nonetheless passes, that's a major falsification and
suggests the sum of simple policies can be easier than any one.

## Motivation

CLOCK-Pro has been adopted in NetBSD, Apache Traffic Server, and
various database buffer caches precisely because it delivers
LIRS-quality hit rates without LIRS's implementation complexity.
The question here is whether the LLM can hold this much structure
in its head while actually doing the task.
