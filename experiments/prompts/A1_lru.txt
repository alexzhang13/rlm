MEMORY-MANAGEMENT HINT — LRU (Least Recently Used):

You accumulate intermediate variables across iterations. Your REPL state
has a soft token budget — when it grows large, you may want to prune.

One heuristic you could consider: when deciding which intermediate variable
to discard (e.g., `del var_name`), you might prefer the one you have NOT
touched in the most recent iterations. You could track a mental `last_used`
ordering for your intermediate variables and consider evicting the
oldest-touched first.

This heuristic is motivated by temporal locality — in many workloads,
things you just used, you tend to use again. It is the LRU policy from
cache theory.
